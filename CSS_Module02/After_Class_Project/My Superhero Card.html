Spider Man

The Origin: Bitten during a field trip, Peter gains superhuman strength, agility, the ability to cling to walls, and a precognitive "spider-sense" that warns him of danger. He sews his iconic red-and-blue suit and builds wrist-mounted web-shooters.

Strenght

Standard Lift: Marvel's official handbook profiles categorize Peter Parker's strength as "Class 10," meaning he can press approximately 10 tons or roughly 20,000 pounds overhead.

Proportional Power: His strength stems from proportional, augmented muscle tissue rather than bulky size, which scales him well above street-level peers like Captain America (who maxes out around 1,200 lbs).

Weakness

Ethyl Chloride: A pesticide and chemical agent famously used by Spencer Smythe in his Spider-Slayer robots to incapacitate Spider-Man.

Spider-Sense Disruption: If Peter's Spider-Sense is blocked by specialized tech, drugs, or a lack of concentration, he loses his primary warning system. Additionally, symbiotes (like Venom) were once bonded to Peter, allowing their hosts to bypass his Spider-Sense entirely.
